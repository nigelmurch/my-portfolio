Case Study 1: Wild Boar to the Rescue

Above image is courtesy of Adobe stock https://stock.adobe.com
southern Italy, wild boar populations have exploded due to climate change and habitat loss. Local foraging groups and chefs have teamed up to harvest wild boar sustainably, turning them into nutritious meals distributed through food banks. This approach not only reduces crop damage and road accidents, but also provides iron-rich protein to vulnerable communities. Controlled harvesting is helping rebalance ecosystems and redirecting meat costs to where it's needed most.

Case Study 3: Flavours By Papatūānuku

Turning wild and invasive species into eco-conscious delicacies, this project reframes perception…

Learn More

Identity:

Earthy colour palette and practical appearence, symbolising “care and focus on whats important” in climate action.

Real Stories: User-generated tasting tales from rangatahi, whānau, and eco-warriors help break down the fear of trying something new.

Experiments: One example? My wild pork cured with whiskey and mānuka honey—tested on 15 participants with 100% saying they’d eat it again!



from the Field

“I thought it’d taste weird. Turns out—it’s better than supermarket ham!” “I finally get why eating invasive species helps the land. That’s genius.” “The retro vibe makes it feel fun, not preachy.”



Outcomes

  • Increased interest from local foraging groups and food educators
  • Workshop invite from sustainable living collectives
  • Supported a values-based conversation about kai sovereignty and kaitiakitanga
  • Created a platform where people don’t just read—they act

I Learned

  • Design is a bridge—between knowledge and action, taste and tradition
  • Cultural narratives matter—Te Ao Māori values must lead, not follow
  • People follow people—real stories > polished marketing

Future Goals for Flavours By Papatūānuku

My future goal 1: I’m planning to be working on interactive maps, forager profiles, and a give back page to help those in need. My future goal 2: bring the experience into classrooms and communities.



to Action

Forage with care. Eat with Purpose. Restore Balance. Flavours by Papatūānuku is just getting started—and everyone has a role to play.
