FLAVOURS BY PAPATŪĀNUKU

Eat wild. Protect nature. Share kai.

Service Design Purpose

Flavours By Papatūānuku is an eco-conscious food design service that connects people to wild and invasive foods—like goat, rabbit, and deer—as a way to nourish communities, reduce food costs, and protect native ecosystems through ethical eating.

  • Environmental Guardianship (Kaitiakitanga): Eating invasive species helps restore balance to Aotearoa's environment and reduces reliance on imported foods.
  • Community Nourishment (Manaakitanga): The service supports those suffering under the weight of rising food costs by offering alternative, nutritious kai.
  • Regenerative Mindset: Instead of just eradicating pests, it reimagines them as part of a restorative, circular food system.
  • Food Culture Shift: Retro visuals help reduce food neophobia and spark curiosity around future foods.
  • Indigenous-Led Values: Grounded in decolonisation, whanaungatanga, and respect for Papatūānuku.

The result is a playful, provocative, and practical way to shift food culture toward sustainability and equity in Aotearoa.

Design Insight

This project is grounded in decolonisation, sustainability, and whanaungatanga. It aims to normalize consumption of wild kai as an act of kaitiakitanga and manaakitanga.

What is F.B.P?

A research-led food design concept encouraging New Zealanders to eat wild, invasive species like deer, goat, and rabbit — not just to reduce their food costs, but to help heal the ecosystem.

Who is it for?

Eco-conscious families, hunters, foragers, foodies, and anyone passionate about sustainability and challenging food neophobia. Especially those struggling with rising costs of living.

Why Retro Design?

To engage users emotionally and visually, using a familiar visual language from old-school games and early web nostalgia — perfect for challenging the way we see "future food."

Where to Next?

Future development includes an interactive recipe book, a forager’s AR guide, and partnerships with iwi and conservation groups to source and cook invasive species ethically and legally.
