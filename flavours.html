FLAVOURS BY PAPATŪĀNUKU

A co-design UX research project exploring food sovereignty, cultural expression, and Papatuanuku’s wisdom in the digital space.

Explore the Project

The Problem!

The problem I wanted to solve is why people are hesitant to trying alternative food sources like invassive flora and fauna, overcoming this leads to exploring the intersection of indigenous identity, seasonal kai, and storytelling.

Secondary Research

My Aim is to design an interactive platform which will allow me to begin anchoring modern design within Māori values and food sovereignty movements while still focusing on delivering the alternative food source to eco-concious consumers and those who are in financial strife.

Initial Research

Taking note of body language and using other observation methods while conducting surveys and interviews to gauge and center authentic voices and lived experiences into viable data I could then use to develop my research further.

Case Studies

My challenge was to start comparing ethical design practices in cultural spaces and food storytelling platforms. Which would then influence my design process.
