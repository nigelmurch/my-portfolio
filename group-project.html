Whetu Marama

Whetu Marama

Reflection of the Project

Whetu Marama was a chance to explore the stars through a user-centred lens—connecting mātauranga Māori with design in a way that inspired and informed. As a design team, we wanted our mahi to shine a light on stories not often told, and to ensure those stories were crafted with respect, playfulness, and authenticity.

Retro aesthetics were used to echo the timeless nature of these narratives, while interactivity and colour ensured our approach felt accessible and bold. This project reinforced my belief that cultural storytelling deserves digital platforms that honour both history and user needs equally.

Open Door Project

Open Door Project

Reflection of the Project

Open Door Project explored access and empathy. We brought design to the streets—literally—with a mobile solution shaped by young people's voices. The goal was to offer not just information, but real, barrier-free access to care and support.

Interactive elements and strong color helped grab attention, while familiar language and safe design made the approach feel approachable. The project taught us a lot about equity in design—and how accessibility starts with listening.
