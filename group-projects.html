Why I Enjoy Working In A Team/h2>

I enjoy working in a team because it allows for a mix of perspectives and skills that can lead to more thoughtful, well-rounded outcomes. I find value in being part of a group where ideas can be challenged, built on, and refined through collaboration. It’s not just about dividing tasks — it’s about learning from others and pushing the work further than I might on my own.

In team settings, I like to contribute by being dependable, actively listening, and helping keep momentum going. I’m someone who pays attention to the needs of the project and my teammates — whether that means stepping up to lead a section, offering a second opinion, or quietly managing details behind the scenes. I’m not the loudest voice in the room, but I’m consistent, collaborative, and committed to doing my part well.

I also enjoy finding ways to connect different contributions into a cohesive whole — whether that’s through communication, design, or simply helping clarify next steps. I’ve found that strong teamwork often comes from respect, trust, and giving everyone space to bring their strengths to the table. For me, it’s rewarding to see how a shared goal can bring people together and produce something none of us could have achieved alone.

Whetū Mārama – Rangatahi Sexual Health Bus

This project focused on reducing stigma and increasing access to sexual health services for rangatahi aged 15–25. We co-designed a mobile clinic experience including a vending machine for free testing kits and private soundproof booths for telehealth consultations.

Open Door Project – Grief and Communication

The Open Door Project explored ways to support tamariki and whānau in talking about grief and loss. Our team created a retro-inspired interactive storytelling experience with resources designed to foster safe and meaningful conversations between children and adults.
