Proclamation of Purpose and Practice

Nigel Murch – UX Researcher & Service Designer

Nigel Murch — UX Researcher & Service Designer

“Your users have secrets, I find them.”
(ethically, of course)

Kia ora, I’m Nigel Murch — a UX researcher dedicated to uncovering what people truly need, especially in spaces where silence, stigma, or systems make access unfair.

Over the past three years, while studying at Media Design School in Tāmaki Makaurau (Auckland), Aotearoa New Zealand, I'm driven by a singular purpose:

To uncover and understand what people truly need — especially in spaces where silence, stigma, or systemic barriers have made access unjustifiably difficult.

I see UX not just as a profession, but as a vehicle for creating a fairer, more compassionate world.

My work is guided by three core principles:

  • Understand deeply
  • Design with purpose
  • Always advocate for the user

As a UX researcher and service designer, I navigate the deeply human side of design — looking beyond surface-level data to discover the emotional, cultural, and systemic layers that influence how people experience services.

Whether I’m co-designing with communities, interviewing rangatahi, or testing early prototypes, my purpose remains constant — to amplify marginalised voices, bridge access gaps, and champion inclusive, ethical innovation.

My practice is shaped by a life steeped in storytelling, deep listening, and collaborative change-making.

I come from the Rural South West of Te Waipounamu (South Island), and because of my lived experiences, my values are firmly rooted in service, curiosity, and integrity.

I believe that when we approach design with empathy and respect people as the experts of their own lived experiences, we elevate design from functional to transformational.

I’m passionate about global equity and access, from sexual health to digital inclusion. There are no excuses for excluding or discriminating against anyone for any reason.

I strive to challenge manipulative design and harmful defaults by promoting transparency, cultural insight, and co-design.

My design philosophy is also grounded in environmental responsibility, believing that digital experiences should be intuitive, inclusive, and sustainable.

My research-led approach transforms complexity into clarity, crafting interactions that feel effortless, respectful, and genuinely useful.

Looking ahead, I’m committed to contributing to bold, community-led projects that uplift lives, whether in healthcare, housing, public services, or digital platforms.

I want to continue supporting organisations in transforming meaningful insight into real-world impact.

I myself have been in the systems of health and welfare, navigating life with a spinal injury that altered my world forever. I’ve felt the cold disconnection of outdated systems not designed to see me — just another number in a waiting room, another checkbox on a form. I’ve faced the humiliation of being judged for asking for help, and the crushing weight of decisions made without me, about me.

That experience carved a scar — not just physically, but in my understanding of how design, when done poorly, can disempower. But it also sparked a fire. It’s why I do this work: to stand up for those left behind, to ensure no one has to feel small, invisible, or unworthy in moments when they need care the most.

This portfolio is more than a collection of work. It’s a reflection of who I am as a designer and a person — someone who believes in equity, ethics, and the power of listening. Someone who shows up with purpose and walks alongside others to build better, together.

“Media, when used with intention and heart, can be a tool for justice.
That’s how, media should create real change, by designing from the heart”.
Ngā mihi nui,
Nigel Murch
Specialist UX Researcher & Service Designer
