Open Door Project

About the Open Door Project

The Open Door Project was a community-focused initiative aimed at improving equitable access to sexual health services and information for young people. Rooted in ethical research practices and inclusive engagement, the project centered the voices and lived experiences of diverse individuals across gender, ethnicity, and sexual orientation.

Our goal was to better understand the barriers young people face when accessing STI testing and sexual healthcare. Through respectful, voluntary participation, we conducted in-depth interviews and gathered insights from students of varying backgrounds, ensuring cultural sensitivity and privacy at every stage.

The project embraced a non-judgmental, harm-reduction approach and actively worked to reduce stigma around sexual health. All data was collected with full informed consent, and participants’ autonomy, confidentiality, and dignity were upheld throughout.

By amplifying youth perspectives and highlighting systemic challenges, the Open Door Project seeks to contribute to more accessible, inclusive, and person-centered sexual health services in Aotearoa New Zealand and beyond.
