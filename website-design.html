Why I Am Passionate About Flavours by Papatūānuku

As I see Flavours by Papatūānuku is a reflection of who I am, what I value, and the kind of future I want to help create. As someone with a deep connection to both the natural world and the people of Aotearoa, I feel a personal and cultural responsibility to protect and celebrate our environment. This project combines my passions for sustainability, design, storytelling, and social change in a way that feels not only urgent but deeply meaningful, and if we want to change the world, then change starts with home. At its very heart, Flavours by Papatūānuku is about restoring balance and It’s about reimagining our relationship with food, not as passive consumers, but as active kaitiaki (guardians) of the whenua (country). Invasive species threaten our ecosystems and biodiversity—yet, ironically, they can also provide nutritious and delicious solutions if approached creatively, yes, you heard that right. By encouraging people to incorporate wild and invasive species into their diets, this project transforms an environmental problem into a cultural opportunity for reconnection, resilience, and innovation.

My passion stems from a drive to challenge the status quo, to flip the narrative around food fear and food waste, and to inspire others to embrace unfamiliar flavours with curiosity rather than resistance. I believe food is one of the most powerful tools we have to bring people together, spark conversation, and drive change. Every recipe, story, and interaction through Flavours by Papatūānuku is designed to educate, empower, and excite people about the possibility of living more lightly and consciously on the land, and in the process, they have a little bit more financial stability, and in some cases, we can give the bounty to those who are suffering in poverty, so nothing will go to waste. What fuels the fire in me is seeing eco-conscious individuals—from whānau to foragers to chefs—start to shift their thinking and embrace new ways of eating that honor both tradition and transformation. My design background enables me to translate this vision into compelling experiences, but it’s the kaupapa that gives it life. This project is a wero (challenge) to all of us: To think critically about the impact of our choices, to celebrate the gifts of Papatūānuku, and to imagine a future where sustainability isn’t a compromise, but a way of life.
